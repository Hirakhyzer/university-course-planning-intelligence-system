# Methodology

The lab models academic planning as a transparent decision-support workflow.

1. Generate fictional course catalog, professors, sections, students, and transcripts.
2. Identify each student's remaining degree requirements.
3. Evaluate prerequisite eligibility for offered sections.
4. Score candidate courses using prerequisite status, capacity, professor availability, course level, and bottleneck pressure.
5. Select advisor-reviewable next-term courses while avoiding time clashes and credit overload.
6. Estimate graduation risk from remaining credits, GPA pressure, course bottlenecks, and limited credit load.
7. Audit recommendation access and risk gaps across synthetic groups.
8. Write reports, figures, and a hash-chained audit log.

The system uses transparent rules rather than hidden automatic decision-making. Real deployments should compare outputs with historical advising decisions and student success outcomes under approved privacy and ethics protocols.
